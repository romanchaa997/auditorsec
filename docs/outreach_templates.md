# AuditorSEC — Outreach Templates
**For first contact with crypto founders, OTC desks, crypto lawyers**

## Strategy

All templates follow the same principle from the interview script:
**Do not sell — verify the hypothesis, offer value.**

Goal: 30-min call → understand their pain language → validate our focus areas.
Offer: Free Security Health Check (2-3 hrs) for first 2-3 projects.

## Interview Script (30-40 min)

**Format:** Zoom / Telegram call
**Goal:** Not to sell, but to hear client language, test hypothesis

### Opening (2-3 min)
> "Thank you for agreeing to talk. I'm currently building a service for crypto
> projects in Ukraine — focus on compliance, asset security and working with
> regulators. I want to first understand how this looks from your side, not
> tell you about myself. No sales — just 5-7 questions. OK?"

### Block 1: Context (5 min)
1. Tell me briefly — what's the project, what market, how big is the team?
2. Are you legally registered in Ukraine or abroad? Why?
3. Which jurisdictions do you currently use for operations / asset storage?

### Block 2: Pain and risks (10-12 min)
1. Which compliance questions take the most time or nerves right now? (let them speak, don't suggest)
2. Were there situations where you didn't know the right way from a legal/security perspective? What did you do?
3. If DFRR or DBR came with questions to your company tomorrow — are you ready? What worries you most?
4. How do you currently store keys / wallets? Who on the team has access?

### Block 3: Current solutions (5-7 min)
1. Have you contacted lawyers or security consultants? What did you get?
2. What was unsatisfying — too expensive, too generic, don't know crypto?
3. If there was a tool/service that solves this "in 2 days" — what should it do first?

### Block 4: Wrap-up (3 min)
1. If I prepare a short report "Top-5 risks for crypto projects in 2025-26 in UA" — send it to you?
2. Is there anyone else in your circle to talk to on this topic? Can you introduce?
> "Thank you — this was very valuable. I won't come with a sales script,
> but if I make something useful — I'll definitely share."
